For You üíï

You are the careful change in air,
the pause before the trees begin again.
Nothing announces itself,
yet the ground softens all the same.
Small things turn toward where you are,
as if beginning learned your name.
What once stayed closed loosens slowly,
and does not return the same.

You are the long light that refuses to leave,
the heat that settles under skin.
Days stretch thin thinking of you,
sleep forgets how to begin.
Shadows stay awake longer now,
even silence learns to remain.
Want grows quietly, without hurry,
like thirst that does not complain.

You are the rain that changes weight,
first a touch, then everywhere.
Thoughts grow dense, the hours slow,
nothing dries in your care.
Roads remember where you pass,
windows hold your sound within.
What enters gently stays completely,
and soaks the ribs and bone and skin.

You are the balance after excess,
the air that knows what to keep.
What falls away was never yours,
what remains is chosen, deep.
Love learns shape, not urgency,
it sits, it breathes, it stays.
No need for proof or promises,
only quiet, steady days.

You are the warmth that asks for nothing,
the room where cold makes sense.
Stillness rests without effort here,
time loosens its defense.
If meaning ever seeks a place
to settle, calm and true,
it will not look beyond this point.
It will stop,
with you.
